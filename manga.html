The history behind Manga

Here we will show you a little, how Manga came to be.

Manga first originated from emakimoto, these are illustrated handscrolls and are dated back to the 12th century.

During the Edo period (1603-1867), a book of drawings titled Toba Ehon further developed what would later be called manga.

It is suggested, that kibyoshi, picture books from the late 18th century, may have been the world's first comic books.

A big growth of artistic inspiration occured in the post-war-period. Thus a lot of earlier Manga-works depict wars or the times right after the war.

Boys and young men became some of the earliest readers of manga after World War II.

Between 1950 and 1969, an increasingly large readership for Manga emerged in Japan with the solidification of its two main marketing genres.

These two genres were shōnen Manga, directed at boys and shojo Manga, directed at girls.

By the turn of the 21st century, manga "achieved worldwide popularity"

A few interesting facts about Manga

Here you can read about a few interesting facts about Manga.

  • Why Manga characters have so large eyes:

    • This universal trend was started by Osamu Tezuka, creator of the Manga "Astroboy".

    Specifically, it sprang from his love of Walt Disney animation, especially the movie "Bambi".

    Because Tezuka's work is a foundation of contemporary Manga, this trait became a defining feature of its character designs.

  • The bestselling Manga of all time:

    • That price goes to "One Piece".

    This Manga sold over 500 million copys worldwide, as of August 2022.

    The Manga "Dragon Ball" follows closely on the 2nd place.

  • The right way to read Manga:

    • Japanese is read from right to left, so naturally, Manga would be read the same way.

    Because of this, the first page of a Manga is in western standards actually the last.

10. Oyasumi Punpun

  • Author:

    Inio Asano

  • Genre:

    Drama, Slice of life

  • Release day:

    March 15, 2007

  • Anime Adaptation:

    No

  • Description:

    Punpun Onodera is a normal 11-year-old boy living in Japan. Hopelessly idealistic and romantic, Punpun begins to see his life take a subtle—though nonetheless startling—turn to the adult when he meets the new girl in his class, Aiko Tanaka. It is then that the quiet boy learns just how fickle maintaining a relationship can be, and the surmounting difficulties of transitioning from a naïve boyhood to a convoluted adulthood. When his father assaults his mother one night, Punpun realizes another thing: those whom he looked up to were not as impressive as he once thought. As his problems increase, Punpun's once shy demeanor turns into voluntary reclusiveness. Rather than curing him of his problems and conflicting emotions, this merely intensifies them, sending him down the dark path of maturity in this grim coming-of-age saga.

6. Slam Dunk

    Takehiko Inoue

  • Genre:

    Sports

  • Release day:

  • Anime Adaptation:

    Yes

  • Description:

    Hanamichi Sakuragi, a tall, boisterous teenager with flame-red hair and physical strength beyond his years, is eager to put an end to his rejection streak of 50 and finally score a girlfriend as he begins his first year of Shohoku High. However, his reputation for delinquency and destructiveness precedes him, and most of his fellow students subsequently avoid him like the plague. 3. Vagabond

  • Author:

    Takehiko Inoue

  • Genre:

    Action, Adventure

  • Release day:

    September 3, 1998

  • Anime Adaptation:

    No

  • Description:

    In 16th-century Japan, Shinmen Takezou is a wild, rough young man, in both his appearance and his actions. His aggressive nature has won him the collective reproach and fear of his village, leading him and his best friend, Matahachi Honiden, to run away in search of something grander than provincial life.
